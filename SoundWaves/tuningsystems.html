Tuning Systems: Just and Equal Temperament

Every note in music has a corresponding frequency. For example a middle C in piano has a frequency of 261.63 Hz.

However, these frequencies might change according to the tuning system used. There are many tuning systems, but two of the most used ones are equal temperament tuning system and just tuning system. In “Just Tuning System” the frequencies of the notes depend on the natural harmonic series. So there is a common ratio between the first and the third or fifth note of a scale. For example, the fifth note in A Major scale is E. To obtain E, the frequency of A must be multiplied by 1.5. However, this tuning system makes some tones sound very pleasing to us while others sound out of tune. In keyboards this is a huge problem as you cannot constantly change the tuning of your instrument. Therefore, a new tuning system named “equal temperament system” is invented. This system takes an octave (to obtain an octave the frequency of the lower note is doubled), and divides the octave to 12 equal frequencies. So, every tone is equally pleasing to ear. Fun Fact: Some people argue that the equal temperament system ruined the harmonies since the thirds and fifths aren’t the exact harmonics of the first note.
