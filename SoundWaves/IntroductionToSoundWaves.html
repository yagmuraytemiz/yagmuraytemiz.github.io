Introduction To Sound Waves

How do sound waves travel?

When a disturbance is caused to a medium, the particles start to vibrate and interact to disturb the nearest particles to them. This causes the particles to be displaced and energy to be transferred. As a result of this process we hear sound. Sound waves are longitudinal waves as the particles vibrate parallel to the direction the sound is travelling. Frequency, Period, Amplitude and Wavelength

The period of a wave is the amount of a time it takes for a wave to travel a distance of one wavelength by definition. And the wavelength of a wave is the distance between the two highest points of the wave ot the two lowest points of the wave. Period and wavelength can sometimes be confusing because they are obtained from the graphs in the same way; however, they are obtained from different graphs. The period of a wave can be determined by analyzing a wave graph with respect to time while the wavelength can be determined by analyzing a wave graph with respect to distance. The frequency of a wave is the reciprocal of the period. Therefore, a greater period means a smaller frequency. The period of a wave is measured by seconds according to the SI Unit system. Since the frequency of a wave is the reciprocal of the period, the unit of frequency is s-1. However, instead of using s-1, scientists use a special name: Hz to measure frequency.
